      Applications

    • Cardiovascular & cardiometabolic illnesses - Assess polygenic predisposition for heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes.
    • Neurogenomics - Estimate the multi-gene predisposition to intellectual disability, ADHD, and certain mental health conditions.
    • Rare diseases with complex presentation - Have complex unsolved disease cases with potentially non-monogenic phenotypes like idiopathic intellectual disability, or unexplained hyperlipidemia? Use our analysis to determine if there is a polygenic explanation.
    • Expand test capabilities - Already gather genetic data for gene panel or SNP array testing? Run it through our pipeline for a more in-depth analysis.
    • Integrate precision medicine into your practice - PolyVar enables you to view the most cutting-edge insights into your patients.
    • Insights into your own data - View your predicted polygenic predisposition for height, red hair, ease of skin tanning, and the disease predispositions mentioned above.
    Our minimum requirements:
    1. The polygenic score must perform better than all other scores available for that phenotype. If we don't have the absolute best score, we don't offer it.
    2. The polygenic score should demonstrate non-negligible predictive power in a sample different from the same in which the polygenic score was derived.
    3. The polygenic score should demonstrate an improvement in risk prediction over an existing model if assessing healthy individuals. For example, a polygenic score which predicts heart disease wouldn’t be as useful if all it only predicts obesity, which can be observed directly.
    4. The polygenic score should be evaluated in multiple ancestry groups.
    5. The polygenic score must be sourced from data generated with controls, such as geographical allelic patterns, the genotyping array, or age.
